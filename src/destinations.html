Destinations

Find the perfect snowboarding resort

Mt. Hood Meadows

With 2,150 acres and 2,777 vertical feet of mountain enjoyment, Mt. Hood Meadows is only 90 minutes from Portland. Beginners may practice their abilities on the easier slopes while more experienced riders can plunge down Heather Canyon or go through glades. The 18-foot superpipe is a highlight of Meadows' several snowboard terrain parks, which are well worth checking out.

Park City Mountain Resort

Park City, billed as the biggest ski resort in the nation, has more than 7,300 acres of terrain, including more than 330 routes, 14 bowls, an Olympic-sized halfpipe, and eight terrain parks. In spite of the fact that the bulk of the terrain in this area is best suited for intermediate and expert snowboarders, beginners are invited to improve their abilities at the Ski and Snowboard School, which provides both private and group training for snowboarders of all ability levels.

Visit Website

Mammoth Mountain

Mammoth Mountain is unquestionably the snowy playground of every snowboarder's dreams, receiving 400 inches of snowfall and 300 days of sunlight annually. The winter wonderland, which is located at 11,05 feet above sea level and offers 3,500 acres of snowboarding terrain, has a season that lasts until July (yup, July). There are many activities outside snowboarding, such as exciting snowmobile excursions, breathtaking gondola rides, snowcat tours, bowling, and indoor golf.

Visit Website

Telluride Ski Resort

Telluride, a hidden mountain resort in Colorado, has a variety of slopes that are perfect for both new and experienced snowboarders. Beginners may hone their abilities on the forgiving slopes of Ute Park or Prospect Bowl, while intermediate and advanced riders can tear down the Plunge or walk to the 13,000-foot Palmyra Peak's severe terrain. The slopes are quite calm, so there will be more space for you to enjoy yourself if there aren't many people here.

Visit Website

Palisades Tahoe & Alpine Meadows

Snowboarders from all over the world converge on Palisades Tahoe (formerly Squaw Valley) and Alpine Meadows in the winter for their breathtaking vistas of Lake Tahoe and the Sierra Nevada Mountains, to name just two. A halfpipe, six peaks, 29 lifts, 4,000 acres of open bowl riding, 271 days of sunshine per year, and 45 inches of yearly snowfall are all present there.
